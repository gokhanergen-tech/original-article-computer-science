## ðŸ“Œ 1. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)

- **Year:** 1995
- **Authors:** Kennedy & Eberhart
- **Original Paper:** [Link PDF](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/488968)

**Summary:**  
Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) is a population-based stochastic optimization algorithm inspired by **bird flocking or fish schooling**:

- Each particle represents a candidate solution.
- Particles move through the search space influenced by their own best position and the best-known position of the swarm.
- PSO is efficient for continuous, nonlinear, and multi-dimensional optimization problems.

**Summary:**  
ACOR extends classical Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) to **continuous domains**:

- Uses artificial ants to probabilistically explore solutions.
- Maintains a **solution archive** to guide the search.
